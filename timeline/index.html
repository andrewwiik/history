James Madison

Birth

James Madison Junior is born on March 16, 1751, the oldest of twelve children to Nelly and James Madison.

March 16, 1751

Madison Graduates

From 1762 Madison studied away from home, returning to the new family house, Montpellier, at age sixteen to prepare for college. In 1769, he enrolled at the College of New Jersey where he enjoyed debating. Madison graduated two years later.

1771

Madison served in the Virginia State Legislature

Madison served in the Virginia state legislature in 1776. He formed early ideas about religious freedom and became known as the protégé of Thomas Jefferson. The pair drafted the Virginia Statute for Religious Freedom, which was passed in 1786.

1776

Madison entered Continental Congress

Madison became the youngest delegate in the Continental Congress in 1780 and gained a good reputation by persuading Virginia to give up northwestern territorial claims to create the Northwest Territory in 1783. Madison is critical of "excessive democracy" and believed legislatures should act in the interest of the wider state.

1780

Madison is hailed as the Father of The Constitution

Madison believed the Articles of Confederation exposed the fledgling country to social unrest, financial debt and foreign attack. Madison persuaded Washington to chair the meeting and he drew up the Virginia Plan which formed the framework of The Constitution.

1787

Madison ensured the Constitution is ratified

The Constitution is ratified in each state, a process which is led by Madison and supported by the publication of his Federalist Papers in New York.

1787

Madison is elected to The House of Representatives

Madison is elected to the House of Representatives in 1789 and introduces the Bill of Rights, guaranteeing civil liberties. He breaks away from George Washington, instead joining Thomas Jefferson to form the Democratic-Republican party. Madison fights the controversial Alien and Sedition Acts.

1789

Madison married Dolley Payne Todd

Madison, who is a shy and retiring a 43-year-old, marries 26-year-old Dolley Payne Todd, a vivacious widow with a young son. Dolley is popular and later makes a name for herself as a wonderful hostess in the White House.

September 15, 1794

Madison is appointed Secretary of State

Madison supported Jefferson's Presidential campaign and served as Secretary of State when the party was victorious. Madison is credited with shaping the foreign policy.
